ABOUT

  • The Toyota Supra is a legendary sports car and grand tourer known for its performance, style, and rich history.

  • 1. History: The Toyota Supra made its debut in 1978 as an offshoot of the Toyota Celica. Over the years, it evolved into a standalone model. The name "Supra" is derived from the Latin prefix, meaning "above," "to surpass," or "go beyond."

  • 2. Generations: The Supra has gone through multiple generations, each with its unique design and engineering. Notable generations include the A40, A60, A70, A80, and the latest generation, the A90 (2020-present).

  • 3. Engines: The Supra has been powered by a range of engines over the years. It is particularly famous for its inline-6 engines, which have become iconic in the world of sports cars. In recent generations, the Supra is powered by a turbocharged inline-6 engine producing impressive horsepower and torque.

  • 4. Performance: The Supra is renowned for its exceptional performance, with quick acceleration and precise handling. It has been used in various motorsport events, including the famous Le Mans 24-hour endurance race.
